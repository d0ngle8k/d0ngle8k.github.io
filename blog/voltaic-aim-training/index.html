How to Get Good at Aim (Voltaic Enjoyer Edition)

aim-training fps voltaic gaming

The Voltaic System

Voltaic is a benchmarking system. You run tests, get scores, and see exactly which skills are weak. Clicking speed? Bad. Tracking? Bad. Smoothness? Bad.

This clarity is everything. You stop guessing and start training what actually matters.

Consistency Over Intensity

Grinding aim 8 hours a day makes you worse. It builds bad habits. 45 minutes of focused training beats 4 hours of deathmatch.

Train 5 days a week, 45 minutes each. That's 225 minutes per week of deliberate improvement. Results show in 2 weeks.

Tracking vs. Clicking

Tracking is smooth mouse movement. Clicking is precision. You need both. But most people are weak at tracking because they practice clicking.

Voltaic tells you which one you suck at. Train that.

The Routine That Works

Warm-up: 10 minutes of basic tracking.

Main: 20 minutes on your weak points (revealed by Voltaic).

Practice: 15 minutes on game-specific scenarios.

Follow this for 30 days and you'll place higher in every game.

Aim Training Is Boring

But boring is free. Free improvement, free elo, free respect. If you find aim training fun, you're doing it wrong. It's work. Work pays off.
